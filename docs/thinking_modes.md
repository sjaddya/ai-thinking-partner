# Thinking Modes

These modes classify *how a thought is functioning*, not what it is about.
Each thought must belong to exactly one mode.

---

## 1. Rumination

**Definition**  
Repetitive thinking that revisits the same concern without introducing new
information, decisions, or actions.

**Non-definition**  
Not planning, not emotional expression, and not theory-building. If a thought
moves toward a decision or generates new options, it is not rumination.

---

## 2. Planning

**Definition**  
Thinking aimed at deciding actions, sequencing steps, or preparing for future
situations.

**Non-definition**  
Not replaying the past, not analyzing emotions, and not abstract theorizing.
If no concrete next step could result, it is not planning.

---

## 3. Emotional Processing

**Definition**  
Thinking focused on identifying, describing, or sitting with emotions and
internal reactions.

**Non-definition**  
Not problem-solving, not decision-making, and not abstract explanation. If the
thought is trying to fix or optimize, it is not emotional processing.

---

## 4. Theory / Abstraction

**Definition**  
Thinking that builds frameworks, explanations, or generalized models about self,
relationships, or the world.

**Non-definition**  
Not tied to immediate emotion, not about what to do next, and not repetitive
looping. If the thought seeks an immediate outcome, it is not theory-building.
